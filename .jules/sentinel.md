## 2024-07-23 - The Perils of an Overly Restrictive Content-Security-Policy
**Vulnerability:** The application was missing security headers, including a Content-Security-Policy (CSP). My initial attempt to fix this involved adding a restrictive CSP.
**Learning:** A poorly configured CSP can be more harmful than no CSP at all. A restrictive policy risks breaking critical application functionality by blocking legitimate resources. Furthermore, including `'unsafe-inline'` in the `script-src` directive significantly weakens the policy's ability to prevent XSS, reducing its security benefit. It's more effective to implement universally safe headers like `X-Frame-Options` and `X-Content-Type-Options` first, rather than deploying a complex and potentially broken CSP.
**Prevention:** When implementing a CSP, it should be done incrementally, ideally starting in a staging environment with a reporting-only mode. For a codebase with many inline scripts, a full CSP implementation should be treated as a larger refactoring task, not a quick security win. Prioritize simpler, safer headers for immediate impact.
